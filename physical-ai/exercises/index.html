Physical AI Exercises

Exercise 1: Embodied Cognition Analysis#

Embodied Cognition in Robotic Systems

intermediateTheoretical

Instructions:

Analyze a robotic system of your choice and identify how its physical form influences its cognitive capabilities. Consider the relationship between the robot's sensors, actuators, and its ability to understand and interact with its environment.

Expected Outcome:

Students will understand how embodiment shapes cognitive processes in robotic systems and be able to analyze the relationship between physical form and intelligence.

Exercise 2: Simulation vs. Reality Comparison#

Addressing the Reality Gap

advancedPractical

Instructions:

Research a specific example where a robot trained in simulation performed differently in the real world. Analyze the factors that contributed to the performance gap and propose strategies to mitigate the reality gap in future implementations.

Expected Outcome:

Students will understand the challenges of transferring skills from simulation to reality and be able to propose solutions to address the reality gap problem.

Exercise 3: Perception System Design#

Multi-Modal Perception System

advancedPractical

Instructions:

Design a perception system for a mobile robot operating in an indoor environment. Specify the types of sensors needed, how they would be integrated, and how the robot would handle uncertainty in itsæ„ŸçŸ¥ (perception).

Expected Outcome:

Students will be able to design integrated perception systems that handle real-world uncertainty and combine multiple sensor modalities effectively.

Exercise 4: Control Theory Application#

Control System for Manipulation Task

intermediateSimulation

Instructions:

Design a control system for a robotic arm to pick up objects of varying shapes and weights. Consider the feedback mechanisms needed and how the system would adapt to different object properties.

Expected Outcome:

Students will understand the relationship between control theory and physical AI, and be able to design adaptive control systems for manipulation tasks.

Exercise 5: Learning Efficiency Challenge#

Sample-Efficient Learning in Physical Systems

advancedTheoretical

Instructions:

Compare the learning requirements for a physical AI system versus a virtual AI system. Analyze why sample efficiency is critical in physical systems and propose strategies to improve learning efficiency.

Expected Outcome:

Students will understand the constraints on learning in physical systems and be able to propose methods for sample-efficient learning.

Exercise 6: Physical AI Application Design#

Design a Physical AI Application

intermediatePractical

Instructions:

Choose a real-world application (e.g., warehouse automation, elderly care, environmental monitoring) and design a Physical AI system to address it. Consider the specific challenges of the physical environment and how your system would handle them.

Expected Outcome:

Students will be able to design complete Physical AI systems for specific applications, considering environmental challenges, safety requirements, and system integration.

Exercise 7: Safety and Reliability Planning#

Safety Protocols for Physical AI

advancedTheoretical

Instructions:

Design safety protocols for a Physical AI system operating in a human-populated environment. Consider fail-safe mechanisms, human-aware safety measures, and system verification approaches.

Expected Outcome:

Students will understand the critical importance of safety in Physical AI systems and be able to design comprehensive safety protocols.

Exercise 8: Energy Efficiency Optimization#

Balancing Performance and Power Consumption

intermediateTheoretical

Instructions:

Analyze the trade-offs between computational performance and energy consumption in a mobile Physical AI system. Propose strategies to optimize the balance for a specific application.

Expected Outcome:

Students will understand the constraints of energy efficiency in Physical AI systems and be able to design systems that balance performance with power requirements.

Exercise 9: Multi-Robot Coordination#

Cooperative Physical AI Systems

advancedSimulation

Instructions:

Design a system of multiple Physical AI agents that must cooperate to achieve a common goal. Consider communication, coordination, and conflict resolution strategies.

Expected Outcome:

Students will understand the challenges of coordinating multiple Physical AI systems and be able to design effective cooperation mechanisms.

Exercise 10: Learning from Demonstration#

Imitation Learning for Physical Tasks

intermediatePractical

Instructions:

Design an imitation learning system for a physical manipulation task. Consider how the system would observe, learn from, and reproduce human demonstrations.

Expected Outcome:

Students will understand the principles of learning from demonstration in physical systems and be able to design effective imitation learning approaches.
