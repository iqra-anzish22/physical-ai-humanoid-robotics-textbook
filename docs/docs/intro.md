# Welcome to Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

## About This Textbook

Welcome to the **Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Textbook**, a comprehensive educational resource designed for advanced students and educators in the field of robotics and artificial intelligence.

This textbook covers the fundamental concepts, practical applications, and cutting-edge developments in Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics.

## What You'll Learn

In this textbook, you will explore:

- **Physical AI Concepts**: Understanding AI systems that operate in the physical world
- **ROS 2 Framework**: Developing robotics applications with the Robot Operating System
- **Simulation Environments**: Using Gazebo and Unity for robotics simulation
- **NVIDIA Isaac Platform**: Leveraging GPU computing for robotics applications
- **Humanoid Robotics**: Design and control of human-like robots
- **Vision-Language-Action Systems**: Integrating perception, language, and action

## Navigation

Use the sidebar to navigate through the different chapters and modules:

- **Physical AI Concepts**: Fundamentals of Physical AI
- **ROS 2**: Robot Operating System framework
- **Gazebo & Unity**: Simulation environments
- **NVIDIA Isaac**: GPU-accelerated robotics
- **Humanoid Robotics**: Human-like robot systems
- **VLA Basics**: Vision-Language-Action models

## Target Audience

This textbook is designed for:

- Advanced AI and robotics students
- Educators teaching Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics
- Researchers and professionals in robotics
- Anyone interested in embodied AI systems

## Prerequisites

To get the most out of this textbook, you should have:

- Basic programming knowledge (Python recommended)
- Understanding of fundamental mathematics (linear algebra, calculus)
- Familiarity with robotics concepts (helpful but not required)

## How to Use This Textbook

Each chapter includes:

- Clear learning outcomes
- Core concepts and theory
- Practical examples and applications
- Exercises and activities
- References and further reading

## Getting Started

Begin with the Physical AI Concepts chapter to establish foundational understanding, then progress through the modules in sequence or jump to topics of particular interest.

For additional resources, exercises, and updates, visit the companion website and GitHub repository.
